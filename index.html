Seeing "App was denied access to SMS"?

That message isn't CallBeacon failing — it's Android protecting you from sideloaded apps by default. It takes one extra step to allow, and then it's done for good.

What you're seeing

App was denied access to SMS

The app requested access to a sensitive permission which can put your personal and financial info at risk.

It's possible that the app won't work properly without this restricted permission.

Why this happens

Starting with Android 13, the system automatically restricts sensitive permissions — SMS, accessibility, notification access — for any app installed outside the Play Store. It's a built-in safeguard against malware, and it applies to every sideloaded app, not just CallBeacon.

Turning it off for CallBeacon takes under a minute, and you only have to do it once.

1

Open CallBeacon's app info

Go to Settings → Apps → CallBeacon. Or press and hold the CallBeacon icon and tap the "i" info button.

CallBeacon⋮
2

Tap the menu, then allow restricted settings

In the top-right corner, tap the three-dot menu and choose "Allow restricted settings." Confirm with your PIN, pattern, or fingerprint if asked.

Why one engine may still flag it

If you scan CallBeacon, you may see it flagged by a single engine under a generic name like "Trojan-SMS.AndroidOS.Agent." That's not a sign of hidden malware — it's a known heuristic pattern. Actual SMS-fraud malware and a legitimate SMS-automation app both request the exact same Android permissions (SMS + call log), so a handful of scanners flag the permission combination itself rather than anything the code actually does.

Apps on the Play Store rarely trigger this because they carry a store reputation signal these engines trust. A sideloaded APK doesn't have that history yet, which is exactly why we're pointing you to an independent scanner instead of asking you to just trust us.
